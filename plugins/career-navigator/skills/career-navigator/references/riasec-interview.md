# RIASEC interview: question bank and tone

## The core technique, restated

Ask a question, get an answer, then ask **one genuine follow-up** before
moving on — a concrete example, a this-or-that trade-off, or the "why" behind
the stated preference. First answers are often the socially-expected one
("I guess I like helping people" is a very safe thing to say); the follow-up
is where the real signal shows up. If the follow-up contradicts the first
answer, keep both data points rather than smoothing them into one — a
student who says they like "working with people" but whose concrete example
is entirely about doing careful solo work *for* one person at a time is
telling you something specific (probably more S-adjacent-but-not-E, or
leaning C), not being inconsistent.

Keep the tone warm throughout. The goal is a real answer, not a defended
position — if a question ever starts to feel like an interrogation, back off
and reframe it as curiosity ("no wrong answer here, I'm just trying to get a
feel for what actually grabs you").

### Contradiction example

> **You:** "What do you do with a free Saturday, no obligations?"
> **Student:** "Probably hang out with friends."
> **You:** "What does that actually look like — are you all doing something
> together, or is it more just being around each other while everyone does
> their own thing?"
> **Student:** "Honestly probably everyone's on their phone half the time,
> I just don't like being alone."
>
> This is a real, useful answer — it's a weaker Social signal than the first
> line suggested (proximity-seeking, not necessarily people-energized
> activity) and worth noting as such rather than logging a high S score from
> the first line alone.

## Question bank by RIASEC dimension

Pick organically based on how the conversation is flowing — this is raw
material, not a script to read top to bottom. Most real answers touch more
than one dimension; that's expected, not a problem to resolve.

### Realistic (hands-on, physical, mechanical, outdoors)

- "If something breaks around the house, are you the one who tries to fix
  it, or the one who calls someone?"
- "Given the choice, would you rather spend an afternoon outside doing
  something physical, or inside on a screen?"
- "Have you ever taken something apart just to see how it worked?" Follow-up:
  "Did you get it back together?"

### Investigative (analytical, curious, research, puzzles)

- "What's a question you've gone down a rabbit hole researching, just
  because you were curious — not for a class?"
- "How do you feel about a problem that doesn't have one clean, correct
  answer?"
- "Do you like knowing *that* a rule is true, or do you want to know *why*
  it's true?"

### Artistic (creative, expressive, unstructured)

- "Blank page, or a clear set of steps to follow — which do you actually
  prefer, honestly?"
- "Is there something you make or create — music, writing, art, video,
  anything — that nobody's making you do?"
- "Do you like it better when there's one right answer, or when there's room
  to interpret it your own way?"

### Social (helping, teaching, caring)

- "Think of a time you helped someone with something genuinely hard for
  them. What pulled you into that?"
- "Are you the one people come to when they need to talk something through?"
- "Would you rather teach someone something you're good at, or just work on
  your own thing?"

### Enterprising (leadership, persuasion, competition, business)

- "Have you ever talked a group into doing something your way? How'd that
  go?"
- "Do you like being the one in charge of how something turns out, or would
  you rather someone else make that call?"
- "Is winning or competition something that motivates you, or does it stress
  you out?"

### Conventional (organized, detail-oriented, structured, precise)

- "Are you the one who color-codes their notes, or the one borrowing
  someone else's the night before?"
- "Do you like a task with a clear checklist, or does that feel tedious?"
- "When you're in charge of planning something — a trip, an event — do you
  actually enjoy the logistics part?"

### General / cross-cutting (useful anywhere in the conversation)

- "What do you actually do with a free Saturday, no obligations?"
- "What's something you've worked on where you completely lost track of
  time?"
- "Which class do you enjoy versus just tolerate — what's the actual
  difference for you?"
- "What's something that drives you crazy when other people do it wrong?"
  (often reveals standards/values that point toward C or E)

## Recording as you go

Don't wait until the "interview is done" to call `career_update_profile` —
call it after each theme firms up, with your running best estimate. A
partial, honest profile (`riasec_confidence: "low"` or `"medium"`, some
letters still unscored) is genuinely useful state to have persisted if the
conversation gets interrupted; a perfect profile that only exists at the very
end of an uninterrupted session is a fragile design to lean on.
